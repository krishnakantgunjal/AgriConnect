Equipment Maintenance
Maintaining Your Farm Equipment

Proper maintenance extends equipment life and prevents costly breakdowns during critical farming periods.

  • Check fluid levels (oil, coolant, hydraulic fluid)
  • Inspect for leaks, damaged hoses, or loose parts
  • Clean air intakes and radiator screens
  • Check tire pressure and condition
  • Test lights and safety equipment

  • Change oils and filters according to schedule
  • Lubricate all moving parts and bearings
  • Check and adjust belts and chains
  • Inspect electrical systems
  • Check and adjust implements for optimal performance

  • Clean equipment thoroughly
  • Apply protective coatings to prevent rust
  • Stabilize fuel systems
  • Remove and store batteries properly
  • Store in a dry, covered area
